Located near Nakuru town on the floor of The Great Rift Valley, this park surrounds Lake Nakuru. It is best known for its thousands of flamingoes which nest along the shores of Lake Nakuru. The surface of the shallow lake is hardly recognizable due to the continually shifting mass of pink. The best vantage point is from Baboon Cliff.Also of interest is an area of 188km around the lake fenced off as a sanctuary to protect giraffes as well as both black and white rhinos. Other animals that can be found here are waterbucks, lions,pythons, cheetahs,leopards and over 400 bird species. The park marches for 12.1km on the south eastern boundary with Soysambu conservancy which is the only remaining wildlife corridor to Lake Naivasha.



This is a freshwater lake located a few kilometers from the town of Naivasha. The name derives from the local Maasai name Nai'posha, meaning "rough water" because of the sudden storms which can rise. The lake is home to a variety of types of wildlife including over 400 different species of bird, zebras, giraffes and a sizeable population of hippos.



Lies south of Lake Naivasha. The park is known for its wide variety of wildlife and for its scenery. This includes towering cliffs, water-gouged gorges, the Fischer's Tower, Central Tower columns and Hell's Gate Gorge. Wildlife include lions, buffaloes, zebras, gazelles, antelopes, reedbucks, leopards and cheetahs. There are also over 103 species of birds in the park. The national park is also home to five geothermal power stations at Olkaria. The park is also popular due to its close proximity to Nairobi and lower park fees compared to other National parks. Hiking, biking and even camping are encouraged within the park. The main setting of the 1994 film, The Lion King is heavily modelled after the park.



Located south east of Lake Naivasha, this park is home to Mount Longonot, a stratovolcano that is thought to have last erupted in the 1860s. Its name is derived from the Maasai word Oloonong'otmeaning "mountains of many spurs" or "steep ridges". The mountain is home to various species of wildlife, notably plain zebras, elands, lion, leopards, bushbucks, giraffe, Thomson's gazelle, buffalloes and hartebeest. A thick forest lies within the crater of the mountain. The crater rim also provides great scenic views across the beautiful Rift Valley all the way to Lake Naivasha. The Longonot satellite earth station is located south of the mountain. Tourists are advised to bring atleast 2 litres of water per person as they might need much of it to hike the mountain.



Located within Nakuru town, this museum depicts the lifestyle of seasonal settlement by prehistoric people at least 3,000 years old. Artifacts from Hyrax Hill site and other sites in the Central Rift Valley are displayed here. The hill was named after hyraxes which are found in abundance, living in cracks within rocks found in this area.



This is a protected conservancy near Gilgil in the Great Rift Valley of Kenya. It is owned and operated by the local community. It contains diverse habitat including short grass, shrubs and woodlands. Almost 100 indigenous plant species are protected in the conservancy. There are over 300 bird species. Grazing animals include buffaloes, eland, impala, waterbuck, gazelle and zebra. Other species include spotted hyena, caracal, leopard, hippopotamus, aardwolf, honey badger and spring hare. The endangered Rothschild's giraffe is also found there.



Located on coordinates 00042'00"S 36030'00"E and 2km to the East of Lake Elementaita is an African Early Stone Age site to approximately 1 million years ago. The museum is monitored by The National Museums of Kenya. It has a stairway going down to where prehistoric handaxes were discovered. In addition, the museum offers natural trails, camp sites, picnic sites, diatomite mining sites, monumental church building, The Gorge, bird watching and caves. On your visit Kariandusi Pre-historic site you will explore several pre-historic features that include hand axes, fossils, stone tools, volcanic glass knives and many other things.



This is a soda lake near the town of Gilgil and is between Lake Naivasha and Lake Nakuru. The lake is a protected area due to its bird life and has been named as an UNESCO heritage site. At the southern end of the lake are the Kekopey hot springs. Over 400 bird species call the lake home. Elementaita attracts visiting flamingoes. The lake's shores are grazed by zebra, gazelle, eland and families of warthog.



This reserve covers Lake Bogoria and the land immediately surrounding the lake. It is administered by the Kenya Wildlife Service. The lake is geothermically active on the western shore, with geysers and hot springs. The lake is alkaline and has algae which attracts flamingoes which in turn attract predators like raptors. In total, 135 species of bird have been recorded in the lake. Large mammals like kudus, buffalo, zebra, cheetahs, baboon, warthog, caracal, spotted hyena, impala and dik dik call the shore home. Siltation and pollution are threatening the scenic lake.



Located along the Nairobi-Nakuru highway, Limuru view point offers some fantastic views of the Great Rift Valley. Enjoy the panorama here, a spectacular view of the largest valley in the world. A place where you can see all major attractions such as Mount Longonot and the escarpments.At the viewpoint there are little stalls to get food or drink. It is free to stop and photography is allowed.
